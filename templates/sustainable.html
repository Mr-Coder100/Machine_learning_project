🌿 Sustainable Farming

Awareness & Education on Organic and Sustainable Practices

🌱 What is Sustainable Farming?

Sustainable farming is an approach to agriculture that focuses on producing food in a way that protects the environment, conserves natural resources, and supports the well-being of farming communities — all while remaining economically viable. It balances productivity with ecological responsibility for future generations.

🔑 Core Sustainable Practices

♻️
Crop Rotation

Alternating crops each season to maintain soil fertility, reduce pests, and prevent nutrient depletion naturally.

✅ Reduces need for chemical fertilizers by up to 30%
🌧️
Rainwater Harvesting

Collecting and storing rainwater for irrigation reduces dependence on groundwater and helps in dry seasons.

✅ Saves up to 50% water compared to flood irrigation
🐛
Integrated Pest Management

Using biological controls, resistant varieties, and minimal pesticides to manage pests sustainably.

✅ Reduces pesticide use by 40-60%
🍂
Composting

Turning organic waste into rich compost improves soil structure, water retention, and fertility naturally.

✅ Increases crop yield by 15-20% over time
🌳
Agroforestry

Integrating trees with crops/livestock provides shade, reduces erosion, and creates additional income sources.

✅ Improves biodiversity and carbon sequestration
💧
Drip Irrigation

Delivering water directly to plant roots minimizes waste and ensures efficient use of every drop of water.

✅ Uses 30-50% less water than traditional methods

⚖️ Organic vs Conventional Farming

🌿 Organic Farming
  • No synthetic pesticides or fertilizers
  • Builds long-term soil health
  • Higher market price (premium products)
  • Better for environment & biodiversity
  • Eligible for organic certification premium
🏭 Conventional Farming
  • Higher short-term yields
  • Lower initial learning curve
  • Relies on chemical inputs
  • Can deplete soil over time
  • Lower per-unit market price
